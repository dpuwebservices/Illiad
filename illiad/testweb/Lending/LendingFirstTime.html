First Time User Registration for Interlibrary Loan

To request an item through Interlibrary Loan, you must first identify yourself to the library's ILLiad system. To do this you fill out a registration form using your Web browser.
Registration has three purposes:

  • Entering your institution's information into the ILLiad system means you will not have to supply the information again when making future requests.
  • Having your e-mail and mailing address on file with Interlibrary Loan enables us to notify you promptly and deliver materials accurately when your request arrives in the library.
  • You will have secure access to information about your requests. You will also be able to review the list of items you have requested in the past.

About your Username and Password

During registration, you will be asked to enter your OCLC Symbol and a Password. You will use this when logging on to the ILLiad system in the future.

Symbol
This is your OCLC 3 or 5 letter alpha/numeric code assigned by OCLC.
Password
Choose anything you like. We recommend that you follow good security practice and choose a password that is different from those you use to access other systems.
ILLiad will encrypt your password, so it will remain secure. Only you will know your password.

ILLiad-Accessing Materials

Loans

The general collection is eligible for interlibrary loan for a period of eight weeks with no renewals. Items will be available within 1-2 business days after receiving the request. Items will be shipped via USPS to the borrowing institution unless otherwise specified. If the borrower wishes to pick up the item, an email notification will be sent when the item is ready and may be picked up only from our Lincoln Park location (2350 N. Kenmore Ave, Chicago, IL 60614).

Articles

All articles and book chapters will be scanned and delivered electronically to a valid email address or Odyssey address if provided.
